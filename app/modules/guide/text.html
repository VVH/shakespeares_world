Identifying Text

Expanded, Deleted, or Unclear Text

Cancelled or deleted words are transcribed even when they are crossed out as long as you can make out what letters or words have been cancelled. If you cannot make out the letters, put in dots (periods) for the number of letters that appear to be there.

transcribed as “wilfullie”

Intralineal insertions (words inserted above the line of text) are transcribed. If the caret mark (^) is in the text, transcribe the caret.

transcribed as “that ^ had any”

Miscellaneous

Brevigraphs such as ampersands (& and &c.) and separators such as hyphens (- or =) are preserved as they appear.

Accidental obliterations, flourishes, and line fillers are not represented in the transcription, but can be noted and described using the “Identify Graphic” button (see below).

List of Tags

There are two groups of tags in the transcription window:

  1. First group (Expansion, Insertion, Deletion, Unclear, Superscript)

    Transcribe the text first and then go back and highlight the letters or words and click on the tag to apply.

  2. Second group (Abbreviations)

    Whenever you encounter one of the following abbreviations, simply click on the appropriate tag and the transcription will be automatically added to the transcription box. You can hover with your cursor over each button to view examples of how these abbreviations are written in secretary hand:
